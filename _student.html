Student Paper Competition

Any student or new Ph.D. whose degree was awarded after Jan 1, {{info.year-1}} is eligible to submit a paper. Papers must contain original unpublished research due mainly to the student. The student must be the primary author and the paper may be no more than 10 pages in length. A panel of judges will determine the best papers.

Additional information on submitting a student paper for the competition can be found on the submission page.

Lodging and conference expenses may be awarded, subject to NSF funding. Student paper winners will have priority.

Postdocs are not considered "students" for this competition, nor eligible for student registration fees.

Students seeking support should email the conference coordinator, Annette Anthony (annetteFanthony@gmail.com) for more information.
